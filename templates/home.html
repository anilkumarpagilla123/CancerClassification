Cancer Classification

What is Cancer?

Cancer is a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells. It can affect any part of the body.

Early Detection

Early detection of cancer is crucial for successful treatment. Regular screenings and awareness can help identify cancer at an early stage.

Prevention

Healthy lifestyle choices, such as avoiding tobacco, maintaining a balanced diet, and staying physically active, can help reduce the risk of cancer.







Learning more about cancer

Kidney Renal Clear Cell Carcinoma (KIRC)

What is KIRC?

KIRC is a type of kidney cancer that begins in the cells lining the small tubes in the kidney. It is the most common type of kidney cancer, characterized by clear cells under a microscope.

Prevention

To reduce the risk of KIRC, it is recommended to maintain a healthy lifestyle, quit smoking, limit alcohol consumption, maintain a healthy weight, and stay physically active.

Early Detection

Early detection of KIRC can be challenging as it often does not cause noticeable symptoms in the early stages. Regular check-ups and screenings can help identify KIRC at an early and treatable stage.

Prostate Adenocarcinoma (PRAD)

What is PRAD?

PRAD is the most common type of prostate cancer, which develops in the gland cells of the prostate. It usually grows slowly and may not cause significant symptoms in the early stages.

Prevention

To lower the risk of PRAD, maintaining a healthy weight, exercising regularly, consuming a nutritious diet, and avoiding smoking are recommended. Regular screenings are also essential for early detection.

Early Detection

Early detection of PRAD involves regular prostate-specific antigen (PSA) blood tests and digital rectal exams (DRE). These tests can help identify any abnormal changes in the prostate and facilitate timely treatment.

Colon Adenocarcinoma (COAD)

What is COAD?

COAD, also known as colorectal cancer, starts in the colon or rectum. It typically begins as a noncancerous polyp and can develop into cancer over time. Early detection is key for effective treatment.

Prevention

Preventive measures for COAD include maintaining a healthy weight, adopting a high-fiber diet rich in fruits and vegetables, reducing red and processed meat consumption, limiting alcohol intake, and undergoing regular screenings.

Early Detection

Screening tests like colonoscopies and fecal occult blood tests (FOBT) are essential for early detection of COAD. These tests can identify polyps or abnormal cells in the colon, allowing for early intervention.

Lung Adenocarcinoma (LUAD)

What is LUAD?

LUAD is the most common type of lung cancer, typically found in the outer parts of the lungs. It often develops in non-smokers and is associated with specific genetic mutations.

Prevention

Preventive measures for LUAD include avoiding smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke, minimizing exposure to environmental pollutants, and protecting oneself from occupational hazards such as asbestos and radon.

Early Detection

Early detection of LUAD involves awareness of symptoms such as persistent cough, shortness of breath, chest pain, or coughing up blood. Timely imaging tests, such as CT scans or PET scans, can aid in diagnosis.

Breast Invasive Carcinoma (BRCA)

What is BRCA?

BRCA is a type of breast cancer that has spread beyond the milk ducts into the breast tissue. It can occur in both men and women, although it is more common in women.

Prevention

Preventive measures for BRCA include regular breast self-exams, clinical breast exams, mammograms, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, limiting alcohol consumption, and avoiding hormone replacement therapy.

Early Detection

Early detection of BRCA involves regular mammograms, breast ultrasounds, or breast MRIs. Genetic testing can also identify mutations in the BRCA genes, helping individuals make informed decisions about their health.
